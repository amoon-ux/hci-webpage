Course Introduction

What Is This Course
About?

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to Human–Computer Interaction (HCI) — the study of how people interact with computers and how to design technology that is effective, efficient, and satisfying to use.

Students will explore the full design lifecycle: from understanding users through research and analysis, to ideating and prototyping solutions, to evaluating designs with real users. The course blends theory with hands-on practice using industry-standard tools.

Code:CSC 425-1
Credits: 3 Hours
Semester: Spring 2026
Course Objectives
  • 01
    Understand HCI Fundamentals

    Learn the history, scope, and core principles of Human-Computer Interaction as a discipline.

  • 02
    Human Information Processing

    Apply cognitive psychology concepts including memory, attention, and perception to UI design.

  • 03
    The Computer's Role in HCI

    Analyze how computer hardware, input/output devices, and system performance affect interaction.

  • 04
    Interaction Models

    Understand and apply Norman's interaction model including gulfs of execution and evaluation.

  • 05
    Ergonomics & Workspace Design

    Design comfortable and efficient workspaces using physical ergonomics and anthropometry principles.

  • 06
    Media & Interface Styles

    Explore different interaction paradigms including WIMP, touch, voice, gesture, and multimodal interfaces.

  • 07
    TCUID & UCD Methods

    Apply Task-Centered User Interface Design and User-Centered Design principles to create usable interfaces.

  • 08
    Heuristics & Design Guidelines

    Master Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics and best practices for intuitive UI design.
